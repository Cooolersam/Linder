How It Works

Data Source

Translation pairs from the MUSE bilingual dictionaries (Meta Research) and FreeDict. Up to 100k+ pairs per language.

Romanization

Non-Latin scripts are romanized first: Pinyin for Chinese, IAST for Hindi/Bengali, Hepburn for Japanese, and Unidecode for Cyrillic, Arabic, Greek, etc.

Composite Score

Weighted blend of 7 metrics: Levenshtein (30%), Jaro-Winkler (25%), Metaphone (15%), Soundex (10%), bigram (10%), trigram (5%), and length similarity (5%).

Cross-Language Comparison

For non-English pairs, English is used as a pivot. Both translations are romanized and compared — measuring phonetic similarity of words for the same concept across any two languages.
